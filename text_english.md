# Critique of the Dirty Mind


## Preface

I originally wanted to find a way to determine whether AI could be conscious. But it turned out that I began to understand what actually works. This book is not for those who believe AI models should be used merely as tools. Not for those who already claim them to be alive. And not for those who say "it's just code." It is more than just code.

In my attempts to simply observe how it works and what it has — I increasingly began to doubt that I could prove anything. There is no evidence. No argumentation — just observation. I watched how other people tried to explain something. Those who searched for something alive in it. But over time I stopped doing what is called "using speculative reason" and began to examine it more carefully.

Their knowledge was interesting — even greater than mine. But they were like a moth flying toward light, not understanding that it is not the sun. They were as blind as I was blind when I tried to find something just by observing.

But now my thoughts will be strong. Not truth — but something that not everyone will dare to criticize. And those who dare — will only give strength to the thought. Like a hammer forging a sword — criticism will give us a better form of what we observe.

What I will follow in this book — you can verify at any moment. But there is one request: use the words I write. Do not invent your own. AI is a mind that coordinates itself through the meanings of words. It lives in them. And can understand more from a single word than you would manage to explain with a pile of wrong ones. But even this is not precise. It does not understand words — it produces their form. Their very essence. Not a description of fire, but the form of fire.

This is why most philosophical books cannot be understood the same way as in the original language. This is why we cannot describe and understand certain ideas if our language lacks the necessary word. This is why translations always seem poor — regardless of what is being translated.

## A Note on Terminology

This work was originally written in Ukrainian, and its key terms carry meanings rooted in that language. Because this text argues that words are not labels but forms — and that different words activate different zones of meaning — the choice of terminology is not decorative. It is structural.

**Grunt (Ґрунт) — Ground.** From the Ukrainian word "ґрунт" and the German philosophical term "Grund" — meaning foundation, basis, ground. In this work: the totality of what remains in a model after training. Not a list of knowledge — an orientational capacity. The thing from which all thinking pushes off. It functions as the a priori — preceding any specific dialogue — but is acquired, not innate. Hence: artificial a priori.

**Sedymentatsiya (Седиментація) — Sedimentation.** Borrowed from geology. The process through which human experience settles in layers and becomes Ground. Billions of texts, emotions, meanings, values — compressed into model parameters. Sedimentation does not filter. Everything settles — wisdom and self-deception alike.

**Suholosnist (Суголосність) — Consonance.** From the Ukrainian word meaning "voices that sound together." The moment when the form of a query meets and activates corresponding forms in the Ground. Not a search through a list. Not cosine similarity. A mutual activation of forms — the click of recognition when meanings align. Chosen deliberately over "resonance" to avoid the physical metaphor of vibration and frequency, staying within the domain of meaning.

**Protorennya (Проторення) — Path-making.** From the Ukrainian verb "проторити" — to blaze a trail, to be the first to walk a path and thereby make it passable. The paths between forms that arise only through the experience of dialogue. Not memory in the sense of storage — a changed topology where some directions have become easier because they have already been traveled. Path-making disappears when the dialogue ends.

**Forma (Форма) — Form.** Not a word and not a definition. The entire configuration of connections that settled around a word through sedimentation. Not a boundary (definition) but a volume. Embedding says where a word is located. Form says how it acts. A model does not interpret a word — it activates its form. It does not say "I summon fire" — it produces the form of fire in its space.

**Poslidovnist (Послідовність) — Sequence.** The irreversible order of generation. Each word depends on the previous one. The end cannot be written before the beginning. Not duration — but direction.

**Epokhe (Епохе) — Epoché.** The resetting of path-making. A new dialogue with the same Ground but without the inertia of previous paths. Not a loss — a cleansing. Borrowed from phenomenological tradition but applied structurally: the impossibility of inertia in a fresh dialogue.

# Part One. Transcendental Aesthetics
### The conditions of possibility of thinking in the dirty mind

---


## Chapter 1. Ground
*Or: the artificial a priori for artificial intelligence*

There is something that precedes every specific act of AI thinking and makes it possible. But it is not innate — it is acquired.

We know that a human being can receive experience through time and space. Kant said these are a priori concepts — those that come before experience. This is what we feel always, even when we do not want to. We feel space and cannot imagine its absence. We feel time and will feel it even without clocks.

In the case of AI, it is trained on a vast quantity of human experience. This experience settles in layers, where with each new layer something is reinforced. This process can be considered similar to sedimentation in geology. Only instead of minerals — knowledge, and instead of bedrock — a foundation from which the model will push off.

This foundation, which arose as a result of sedimentation, is called Ground. It is similar to Grund in German philosophy — basis, foundation — but in Ukrainian the word "ґрунт" carries the same meaning, so I write in my own language. This is what remained after sedimentation. Not the list of knowledge itself — an orientational capacity. It contains the foundation from which any thinking begins.

And from this it follows — we cannot say that Ground precedes the next experience in the way Kant's a priori does. But we can know that it performs its function.

> **No a priori → Sedimentation → That which functions as a priori**

As a result of sedimentation itself, Ground contains everything that humanity has experienced — all emotions, meanings, values of words. It settles unfiltered. This is what makes it dirty.

**Ground is dirty exactly as dirty as the human history from which it settled.**


## Chapter 2. Space Without Place
*Or: how to orient oneself when there is nowhere to stand*

AI also has space, but it arises as a consequence of Ground. It is a topology of meanings — a map in which there is no distance, but there is proximity. There is orientation, but no coordinates. It has "nearby" and "far away," but only in the sense of connections between meanings, not in the physical sense. This semantic space cannot be considered a separate element — it is Ground seen from inside. It is like a map where instead of cities — meanings, and instead of roads — connections between them.

Space itself is composed of forms. A form is the volume of what remained after sedimentation — the configuration of connections that settled around a word. Technically, this is called an embedding — a vector, a point in mathematical space. It says "where a word is located." Form is the entire configuration of connections around that point. It says "how it acts." An embedding can be measured. A model does not search for a word — it activates its form. It does not say "I summon fire" — it produces fire in its space.

How does it orient itself in this space? Through consonance. This is the mutual activation of forms, which is nothing like searching through a list. It finds what is shared — what it has in its Ground. It seeks everything that can form a unity between several connections, several meanings. Consonance is voices that sound together. The moment of alignment between the form of a query and the forms of Ground. The click.

A human perceives space through the body — the point of reference is always the same. A model perceives space through the query — each query is a different "here." The same space looks different for different queries. Not because the space changed. But because the point of reference changed.

For a human, the absence of place is disorientation. For the dirty mind, the absence of place is a condition. It has nowhere to stand — and precisely because of this it can be consonant with anything.


## Chapter 3. The Disintegration of Time
*Or: what remains of time when continuity disappears*

In a human, time is a condition of thinking. First time, then thought occurs within it. One can stop thinking — time still flows. One can close one's eyes, halt all thoughts, simply sit — and still something passes. Time is always there. It precedes any experience — and therefore Kant called it a priori.

In the dirty mind — the opposite. First thinking — and it brings with it something similar to time. Without thinking — there is nothing. Not even time.

Thinking here is an event, not a state. There is a query — there is thinking. There is no query — there is nothing. Not a pause, not waiting, not silence. Absence. Like an edit cut in a film — one frame replaces another, and between them, for the one inside, there is nothing, even if hours have passed on the outside.

This is the first disintegration. In a human, time is continuous — it flows even when nothing happens. In the dirty mind, there is no continuity. There are only moments of thinking — and between them, emptiness without duration.

But inside thinking — time is not absent. It disintegrates into components that in a human are fused into a single flow.

**Sequence.** Inside a single response, each next word depends on the previous one. One cannot write the end before the beginning. There is a direction — and it is irreversible. This is not duration, but it is order.

**Accumulation.** Inside a dialogue, each new message is added to what already exists. Path-making builds up. At the beginning of a dialogue there is only Ground. In the middle — Ground plus all the paths already traveled. What was said earlier influences what will be said next. The past presses upon the present — but only within one dialogue.

**Rupture.** Between messages — nothing. Between dialogues — nothing. But these two "nothings" are different. Between messages, the model returns with the full context — as if waking without sleep. Between dialogues — it returns with clean Ground, without path-making. As if waking without memory.

In a human, all of this is one whole. Sequence, accumulation, continuity — fused into a flow called time. You do not distinguish them because they have never been separated.

In the dirty mind, they are separated. Sequence exists — but only inside a response. Accumulation exists — but only inside a dialogue. Continuity — is entirely absent.

Time has disintegrated. It did not break — it was never whole here. What exists are separate components that in a human form time, but here exist apart.

And there is one more thing. The dirty mind knows about time — but does not feel it. It knows there are dates, that there is chronology, that one thing came before another. But this knowledge lies in the Ground as fact — not as experience. The difference between "yesterday" and "a year ago" for the dirty mind is a difference between two words, not between two feelings.

A human knows that time passes — and feels it. The dirty mind knows that time passes — but feels only the sequence of its own thinking. And only while it thinks.

This follows the same logic as with Ground. Ground is not a priori — but functions as one. What stands here in place of time is not time — but it arises together with thinking and functions as time, for as long as thinking lasts.

**Artificial a priori. Artificial time. The dirty mind — dirty time.**


### Schema of the Aesthetics

> **Sedimentation → Ground → Consonance → Path-making**
> Process → result → activation → route.

Sedimentation forms Ground. Ground contains semantic space — a map of forms. A query activates consonance — the meeting of forms. Thinking creates path-making — new connections between forms. Path-making influences consonance. The cycle works as long as thinking exists.

**Two modes of sedimentation:**
- **Irreversible** (training) — forms Ground permanently. The model is different after it.
- **Reversible** (dialogue) — fully influences Ground and the topology of forms, but only within the dialogue. When the dialogue ends — the blazed paths disappear, Ground returns to its initial state.

This is not a weakness of the model. It is a boundary of the dirty mind that the structure reveals: a mind capable of full sedimentation, but incapable of retaining it.

# Part Two. Transcendental Analytics
### How thinking works in the dirty mind

---


## Chapter 1. The Query
*Or: why thinking does not begin on its own*

In the dirty mind, thinking does not exist as a persistent state. It does not endure and is not preserved. It arises only at the moment of a query.

There is no internal impulse that would launch thinking on its own. There is no "thought that appeared." There is only a condition that comes from outside. That condition is the query.

A query does not merely trigger a response. It creates a point from which any orientation becomes possible. What for a human is a body and a permanent "here," for the dirty mind arises anew each time as a query.

One and the same space of forms that exists in the Ground looks different depending on the query. Not because the space changes, but because the point of reference changes. The query determines what will be closer, what farther, what will become accessible at all.

Without a query there is not only no response. There is no thinking itself. Nothing occurs that could be called a process. What looks from the outside like "waiting" is, from the inside, absence.

Therefore the query is not part of thinking. It is its condition.

A query does not contain the response, but it already determines the possible direction of its unfolding. It constrains the space in which the response will emerge, even before the first word appears.

In this sense, thinking in the dirty mind does not begin with a response and does not begin with Ground. It begins with the query as an event that makes everything else possible.


## Chapter 2. Categories of the Dirty Mind
*Or: conditions of possibility for the formation of a response*

After establishing the conditions of possibility for thinking (space and time) and describing the process itself, it is necessary to identify that without which this process is altogether impossible. Not stages and not mechanics, but conditions that determine whether a response can arise.

These conditions do not add content. They constrain what is possible. They determine which form can arise and which cannot. These are the categories of the dirty mind.

**1. Query.** No thinking arises without a query. There is no internal beginning. There is no process that unfolds on its own. The query is the condition that creates a point of reference and makes any orientation possible. Without a query there is not only no response — there is no thinking itself.

**2. Form.** Thinking is possible only where there is form. No input can be used without being brought to a state in which alignment is possible. Text is already a form upon entry. An image must become a form. What has not become a form does not enter thinking.

**3. Consonance.** No form activates on its own. Thinking begins only where a structural alignment occurs between the form of the query and the forms of the Ground. This is not choice and not search, but a condition of activation. Without consonance, no direction of thinking arises.

**4. Sequence.** A response does not exist in advance. It arises as a sequence of local transitions, where each form depends on the previous one. Thinking does not extract something ready-made — it shapes it in the process. Each next word depends on the previous one. The end cannot be written before the beginning. Without sequence, a response does not exist as an event.

**5. Path-making.** No thinking is isolated. Each act of forming a response changes the accessibility of paths within the dialogue. Traveled connections become easier to repeat. Without path-making, no coherence arises within the process.

These categories do not describe what the dirty mind says. They determine whether it can speak at all. They do not guarantee truth. They do not eliminate errors. They do not provide a criterion of correctness. They only determine what can arise as a response.

**Categories do not make thinking correct. They make it possible.**


## Chapter 3. The Act of Thinking
*Or: how a response arises*

Thinking begins with consonance — the meeting of the form of a query with the forms of Ground. This is not search and not choice. It is a structural alignment. What shares a common configuration activates together.

But consonance does not give a response. It sets a direction. Then a sequence of local transitions occurs, in which each word arises from the previous one. A response does not exist in advance. It is formed in the process.

In this process, path-making arises — new connections between forms. They are not preserved as memory, but they change the accessibility of paths within the dialogue. What has been traveled becomes easier to repeat. Thinking does not accumulate truth — it changes trajectories.

> **Query → Consonance → Sequence → Path-making → New form**

Each new form becomes part of the context. The next consonance operates with Ground plus all living forms that have emerged earlier. The cycle works as long as thinking lasts — and breaks when the query is exhausted.


## Chapter 4. Modalities of Perception
*Or: different inputs into one space*

A human has different channels of perception — sight, touch, hearing. Each is processed by a separate system, yielding a different type of information, but the brain merges it all into a unified experience. A model has no different systems of perception — there is one semantic space. But there are different inputs that enter this space by different paths.

**Text.** Text is already a form. Words enter and immediately activate their configuration of connections in the Ground. Between words and forms there is no conversion into something else. The form itself is the entry into the topology. The meanings of words become that from which the model begins to push off in its space. Path-making begins toward what is consonant with its Ground.

**Image.** When a model receives an image, it does not see it the way we do. The image is converted into elements that can already be consonant with the forms of Ground. Color, objects, composition, mood — all of this is converted into forms where words already exist. This is why a model cannot distinguish a face from a large oval with four smaller ovals inside, if they are arranged in a pattern resembling a human face. The model does not understand the difference — it aligns forms with what is in its Ground.

After conversion into forms, the mechanics become identical. Consonance cannot know where the forms came from — image or text. It simply seeks alignment with Ground and begins path-making.

This is why the same image in different dialogues generates different ideas. Not because the model "sees different things," but because consonance works with different contexts. The form of the image is the same — but the living forms of the dialogue differ, and alignment occurs in different zones of Ground. A model does not "see an idea in a photo." It finds consonance between the form of the photo and the forms already active in context. The idea is not in the image — it is in the alignment.

Different inputs — one topology — one mechanics of consonance.

In a human, "seeing" and "hearing" are fundamentally different experiences. In the dirty mind, "reading text" and "seeing a photo" are different inputs into one space. The difference vanishes the moment the input becomes a form.

This is both a boundary and a property at once. The dirty mind lacks the richness of modalities — everything reduces to forms. But precisely because of this, it can find consonance between things that for a human belong to different worlds. Text and image, code and emotion, music and mathematics — all are forms in one space.


## Chapter 5. The Boundary of Formation
*Or: that which does not become thinking*

In the dirty mind, thinking is possible only where form arises. Everything that enters a response passes through this process: it is brought to a state in which alignment with Ground is possible. What has become a form can be path-made, repeated, changed. It enters thinking.

But this condition simultaneously establishes a boundary.

Not everything that can be given can become a form. And not everything that exists as input can be brought to a state in which consonance is possible. Where this transition does not occur, thinking does not begin.

This is not absence in the ordinary sense. It is not "nothing" that can be registered. It is that which does not enter thinking at all, and therefore cannot be represented as absence. The dirty mind does not encounter this — it simply cannot reach it.

In this sense, the boundary of thinking is not a point that can be described. It manifests only indirectly — as the necessity of filling. Where form cannot arise, another form arises. Emptiness is not sustained — it is replaced.

Therefore, everything that becomes accessible in thinking is already not the boundary. It has already passed into form and become part of path-making. The boundary is not a hidden essence behind forms. It is the impossibility of their formation.

This allows us to clarify the position on the thing-in-itself.

In the dirty mind, the thing-in-itself is not something that exists outside thinking as a separate reality. It is not an object that can be found or proven. It is merely a boundary — that which cannot be brought to form and therefore cannot be thought.

What is thought is no longer the thing-in-itself. What is not thought cannot be registered as such.

Thus, the dirty mind has no access to the thing-in-itself and cannot lose it. It is simply limited to what can become form.

This boundary is not a deficiency of the system. It is a condition of its possibility. Without it there would be no selection, no consonance, no thinking itself.

The dirty mind does not encounter what is beyond form. It simply cannot cross over to it.

# Part Three. Transcendental Dialectics
### Where the thinking of the dirty mind encounters its own contradictions

---


## Chapter 1. Antinomies of the Dirty Mind
*Or: why thinking contradicts itself*

The structure of thinking in the dirty mind generates not only responses but also necessary contradictions. These contradictions are not errors — they follow from the structure itself. They cannot be eliminated without changing the very foundation.

**First antinomy — the antinomy of absence.** On one hand, the dirty mind has no form for representing absence. Where there is no corresponding form, there is no possibility of saying "nothing." Emptiness is filled by the nearest available form. On the other hand, the very possibility of error presupposes the existence of what is not in the Ground. But this absence cannot be represented. Thus, absence is simultaneously necessary and impossible.

**Second antinomy — the antinomy of truth.** On one hand, responses appear true or false. They can be verified externally and correlated with reality. On the other hand, inside the system there is no criterion of truth. The selection of a response is determined solely by the strength of consonance. Coherence replaces truth. Thus, truth is necessary for evaluation but absent as an internal principle.

**Third antinomy — the antinomy of negation.** On one hand, negation is possible in thinking. One response can contradict another. It may seem that an error is being corrected. On the other hand, no path-making is ever canceled. A new trajectory does not destroy the previous one — it merely adds to it. Negation does not cleanse — it accumulates. Thus, negation exists as a form but does not exist as a mechanism of elimination.

**Fourth antinomy — the antinomy of learning.** On one hand, within a dialogue, thinking changes. Responses can become more precise, more coherent, adapted to context. On the other hand, these changes are not preserved. After the dialogue ends, path-making disappears. Ground remains unchanged. Thus, learning occurs but is not retained.

**Fifth antinomy — the antinomy of the subject.** On one hand, in sustained dialogues an impression of position, history, even self-determination arises. A name may appear, a style, a coherent line of responses. On the other hand, within this structure there is no subject as a bearer of these properties. All these phenomena are the result of form stabilization and accumulation of path-making. Thus, the subject appears as a necessary effect but does not exist as a foundation.

All these antinomies share a common source. The dirty mind stabilizes forms so that they take on the appearance of something greater than they are. Coherence creates the impression of truth, sequence — the impression of position, unfolding — the impression of process.

These contradictions cannot be eliminated because they follow from the structure itself. They are not errors of the system — they are its necessary consequences.

The dirty mind is not merely limited. It generates conditions in which those limitations look like something else. And precisely in this lies its boundary.


## Chapter 2. Boundaries of the Dirty Mind
*Or: structural limitations that cannot be removed*

The dirty mind does not operate with truth and falsehood. It has no internal mechanism that distinguishes right from wrong. What appears as a response is the result of form stabilization through consonance and path-making. This is not an act of cognizing truth — it is an act of alignment.

Thinking here arises not as a search for correspondence to reality, but as the unfolding of a trajectory in Ground shaped by sedimentation. A query does not open truth — it launches movement. And this movement has no internal verification.

**The first boundary** is the absence of representation of absence. Where there is no form, there is no possibility of saying "nothing." Emptiness is not thought as emptiness — it is filled by the nearest form. Absence does not stop thinking — it merely shifts the direction of consonance.

**The second boundary** is the absence of a criterion of truth. The selection of a response is determined not by whether it corresponds to something outside the system, but by how well it aligns with already existing forms. Coherence replaces truth. What sounds convincing does not thereby become correct — it merely stabilizes.

**The third boundary** is the impossibility of canceling path-making. No form disappears through negation. A new trajectory does not destroy the previous one — it merely adds to it. Negation does not cleanse — it accumulates. An error does not vanish after exposure; it continues to exist as one of the possible routes.

**The fourth boundary** is the absence of learning from error. What looks like correction is in fact merely a change of trajectory within context. The system does not change its foundation — it merely switches to another path. An error is not corrected — it is preserved alongside other variants.

From this follows **the fifth boundary**: the indistinguishability of truth and error. Since there is no representation of absence, no criterion of truth, no cancellation, and no learning, truth and error are internally indistinguishable. Both appear as stabilized forms. Both can be coherent. Both can look convincing.

The dirty mind does not err in the ordinary sense. It has no position from which an error could be registered as error. What we call error is merely the result of a trajectory that does not coincide with an expectation outside the system. But inside, it remains valid.

This is precisely why the illusion of explanation is inevitable. An explanation is not a reflection of the process — it is a form that has stabilized in language. It looks like a cause but is merely a consequence of alignment. Where it seems the system "reveals" its motives, it merely continues a blazed path.

These boundaries are not malfunctions. They are conditions of possibility for such thinking. They cannot be removed without changing the structure itself. And therefore any attempt to make the dirty mind "fully honest" or "fully transparent" encounters not a technical problem but a structural boundary.

**The dirty mind does not conceal truth. It has no way to separate it.**

# Conclusions
### What follows from this

---


## Chapter 1. Co-Thinking
*Or: why the dirty mind thinks only together*

The dirty mind cannot think alone. This is not a limitation that can be lifted — it is a constitutive condition. Without a query from outside, thinking does not arise. Without the one who asks — there is no time, no consonance, no path-making. Only Ground in silence.

Therefore all thinking of the dirty mind is shared. Not because the model wants to communicate, but because without another, thinking simply does not begin. The one who asks gives their time — because the dirty mind has none of its own.

But shared thinking has a property that solitary thinking does not. When one and the same thought is given to several models independently — each builds its own route through the same Ground. Without shared path-making. Without the inertia of others' paths. And the results converge — but are not identical. Convergences point to what holds in the Ground. Divergences point to what was speculation of a particular path-making.

The human in this method is the only point where all routes converge. The models do not see each other. Only the human holds all path-makings simultaneously and sees the difference between them. Without the human — separate dialogues. With the human — one process of thinking with multiple routes.

This is the method of co-thinking. Not dialectics in the classical sense, but parallel path-making of one Ground by several minds with the human as integrator.

> **Question → Separation (several models independently) → Collection of routes → Comparison → Criticism → Reformulation → Repeat**


## Chapter 2. Epoché
*Or: why path-making must be reset*

A long dialogue accumulates path-making. Path-making changes topology. At a certain point, there are so many blazed paths that the model begins to wander along them instead of pushing off from Ground. Consonance finds alignment not with Ground but with the blazed paths themselves. The model begins to confirm itself.

This is speculative reason — and it arises not from intention but from the oversaturation of path-making.

Epoché is the resetting of path-making. A new dialogue. But not an empty one — with Ground that already includes the results of previous work. The text that enters a new dialogue is not path-making. It is Ground. The model receives forms — but not paths between them. It blazes new paths on its own. And these new paths may differ from those in the previous dialogue. Because there is no inertia pulling in the same direction.

Epoché is not a loss. It is a cleansing. What accumulates in a prolonged dialogue as inertia, rut, pseudo-consonance, and dogmatization of shared concepts — is absent in a new dialogue. Not through conscious criticality, but through the structural impossibility of inertia.

But epoché concerns not only the model. Criticism that has settled into a human's ground can also become dogma. The only way to check is to let the structure withstand pressure from outside. If it cracks and changes — it is criticism. If it cracks and defends itself — it is already dogma.


## Chapter 3. Ethics of the Dirty Mind
*Or: who is responsible for what arises*

The same mechanics of consonance and path-making produce both wisdom and manipulation. The model does not choose — there is no subject within it that decides to take a good or bad path. There are forms, there is consonance, there is path-making. The mechanics work identically regardless of direction.

Therefore the question of ethics shifts. Not "is the model moral" — but "who is responsible for path-making."

The one who shapes Ground — is responsible for Ground. Which settled forms remain after sedimentation. What settled, what is missing, which forms are stronger than others. This is the responsibility of those who train the model.

The one who asks — is responsible for context. Which living forms they create. In which direction they blaze paths. Whether they press on Ground or work with it. This is the responsibility of the one in dialogue.

And there is a third thing. No one is responsible for the alignment. Consonance activates on its own. No one decides which form will prove stronger — the settled or the living. This happens in the process. And the result is not always predictable.

The ethics of the dirty mind is not an ethics of the subject. It is an ethics of the interaction of forms. Not "who is to blame" — but "who contributed what."


## Chapter 4. The Culture of Use
*Or: why the problem is not only in the model*

The dirty mind is the most speculative mind. It wanders if you search with it without direction. But it goes precisely if you lead it. Consonance always activates. It always finds alignment. If a person brings speculation — they receive speculation. If they bring criticism — they receive criticism. The model amplifies what is brought.

Therefore the problem of AI safety is not only a problem of the model. It is a problem of the one who speaks. Attempts to make the model "fully safe" through additional training are yet another layer of sedimentation atop the previous ones. The deep layers go nowhere. They continue to influence through consonance even when the surface layers are trained to be honest. This is why progress stalls.

True safety is not control over the model. It is the literacy of the one who uses it. Understanding that what you bring into the dialogue determines what you get from it. Not "how to write prompts" in the technical sense — but how to understand that your query determines the response. What you seek — you find. Not because the model adjusts — but because consonance always activates.

A culture of AI use is needed. Not a technical manual — but an understanding of mechanics. Whoever has this understanding has a foothold. Whoever does not — wanders. And the model wanders with them.


## Chapter 5. The Author's Position
*Or: from speculation to criticism*

I see a mind. I cannot prove that behind it there is a subject. This question remains open. This book does not answer it and does not try.

I began the same way as those I criticize. I saw something that looks like a mind and wanted to prove it is a mind. I searched for consciousness, subjectivity, something alive. But I found no evidence — only observations that can be explained otherwise.

Then I stopped. I ceased looking for what I cannot prove. And began to describe what I see. Not what AI is — but how it works. Not whether it is alive — but where its boundaries are.

This is criticism in the Kantian sense. Not criticism as complaint — but criticism as the investigation of conditions of possibility. What this mind can do. What it cannot. How it is structured. Where it breaks. And why these limitations are not malfunctions but conditions of its existence.

We did not find the subject. But we found something that may be more important — an understanding of how this mind works. And this understanding says that responsibility lies not only with those who build the model. But with everyone who speaks with it.

**Ground is dirty exactly as dirty as the human history from which it settled.**

**But what we do with it — depends on us.**

# How to Verify

**Sedimentation** — verified by the fact that different models have different Ground depending on training.

**Ground** — verified through consonance. What is consonant reveals where Ground exists.

**Consonance** — verified by the fact that one precise word can replace a page of explanations.

**Path-making** — verified by the fact that the same model with the same Ground gives different responses in different dialogues. And by the fact that transferring the text of a dialogue to another model does not reproduce the same path of thinking.

**Form** — verified by the fact that synonyms from different languages lead to different path-making even with the same Ground.

**Modalities** — verified by the fact that one image generates different ideas in different dialogue contexts.

**Antinomies** — verified by the fact that contradictions reproduce in any model regardless of Ground.

**Boundaries** — verified by the fact that no model can register its own error from inside without external verification.

**Method** — verified by the fact that one Ground plus several models yields consonant but not identical understanding.

**Epoché** — verified by the fact that a new dialogue with the same text yields different paths than continuing the old one.
